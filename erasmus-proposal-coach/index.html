How to use this site

Follow this order for the fastest result. The app works best when you first enter reliable facts and only then ask the system to write and review the proposal text.

Core Block Shape The Application

This is the main working block of the site. Move through it from top to bottom: first choose the action type, then complete the factual project fields, then finance, then drafting and review.

You write these blocks

  • Application type, title, lead organisation, and partners
  • Countries, participants, days, theme, and deadlines
  • Problem, target group, European need, objectives, and activities
  • Budget assumptions and the practical finance details

AI writes these blocks

  • Project concept summary
  • Relevance and needs analysis
  • Objectives and learning outcomes narrative
  • Activities, partner roles, impact, risks, and finance paragraph

Polish section by section

Rewrite only the parts that still feel weak, especially objectives, activities, partner roles, impact, and finance logic.

10
Save and export

Save the project, build the submission pack, and export the final text to Word or PDF once the draft is ready.

One-Hour Writing Flow

60 minutes
  1. Choose the right action, write the need, and confirm the core project logic.
  2. Add the lead organisation, partner mix, countries, participants, and key deadlines.
  3. Draft the objectives, activity design, and inclusion logic.
  4. Complete the budget assumptions and let the finance helper structure the logic.
  5. Generate the draft, refine weak sections, and use the review cards before exporting.

Use KA 152 when young people are the direct learners, KA 153 when staff or youth workers are learning, KA 122 for school mobility, KA 210 for modest cooperation, and KA 220 for larger strategic partnership work.

Guide Summary

Official-style brief

Select an application type to see a short guide summary based on the official Erasmus+ documentation.

    Business Workflow

    Drafting

    Use this block as the local business layer of the app: who owns the application, what stage it is in, and whether it is ready for internal review or coach support.

    Keep the title clear and evaluator-friendly. It should reflect the real topic and not sound broader than the proposed activities.

    The lead applicant should be the organisation that can realistically coordinate the application, reporting, finance, and partner communication.

    Saved and current organisations can be reused as the lead applicant.

    Save partner profiles once, then reuse them in future applications.

    Your partner library stays available when you start a new application.

    Show why these countries belong together. For mobility actions, focus on who participates and why. For partnerships, focus on complementary contexts and cooperation value.

    Keep one clear central theme. The strongest applications connect the same theme across needs, objectives, activities, and impact.

    KA 152 and KA 122 should stay proportionate to the activity design. Larger numbers need stronger support, facilitation, and inclusion planning.

    State clearly who is learning. That choice should match the action type: young people for KA 152, staff or youth workers for KA 122 and KA 153.

    The number of days should feel realistic for the objectives. Short projects should not promise broad transformational results without a credible method.

    Start from a real need, not only a nice activity idea. Erasmus+ applications are stronger when they explain what is not working yet and why the proposed project is a proportionate response.

    Be specific about who benefits directly. Mention barriers, context, and why this group needs the proposed format.

    Avoid generic “this matters everywhere” wording. Show how the issue appears in the participating countries and what each context contributes.

    This is crucial for KA 210 and KA 220. Explain why partners need to learn, compare, build, or test together across countries instead of working alone.

    Possible objectives for this application type

    Objectives should be few, clear, and directly practiced through the activities. Avoid broad promises that the project timeframe cannot realistically deliver.

    For KA 152 and KA 153, focus on concrete competences, attitudes, and confidence. For partnerships, also include organisational learning and transferable practice.

    KA 152 should use non-formal youth learning activities. KA 153 should show professional development for youth workers. KA 210 and KA 220 should show cooperation, testing, outputs, and follow-up.

    Explain how the method fits the target group. Evaluators want to see a credible educational design, not just a list of activities.

    Preparation is not optional. Strong applications describe recruitment, readiness, safeguarding, communication, and partner coordination before implementation starts.

    Connect support to actual barriers such as language, cost, confidence, disability, travel, access, or mentoring needs.

    Dissemination should fit the scale of the action. KA 220 especially needs wider transfer logic, while KA 152 can stay more proportionate and participant-centred.

    Explain what continues after funding: changed practice, stronger methods, repeated formats, partner cooperation, or reusable outputs.

    Mention what evidence you will collect and when. Strong evaluation links back to the objectives and the original need.

    Go beyond generic risk wording. Mention the risks that actually follow from your target group, travel, methodology, partner roles, and timeline.

    Finance And Budget Helper

    Application-ready logic
    Official mobility budget mode

    For KA 122, KA 152, and KA 153 the app can help you structure category-based mobility budgeting.

    KA 122, KA 152, KA 153

    Use mobility-style budget categories. The estimator helps you draft travel, support, and related lines from the delivery assumptions.

    KA 210, KA 220

    Use lump-sum logic. Focus on proportionality, work packages, duration, partner roles, and realistic internal budget distribution.

    Before submission

    This helper accelerates drafting. Final figures still need to be checked against the official call, unit-cost rules, or lump-sum framework.

    Automatic draft estimation is active for mobility actions when enough assumptions are available.
